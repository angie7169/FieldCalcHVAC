What Size AC Do I Need? BTU Sizing Guide

Updated July 2026 · By FieldCalc HVAC · 8 min read

Whether you're replacing an old unit or sizing new construction, picking the right AC size matters more than any other decision. Too small and it can't keep up; too big and it short-cycles, dehumidifies poorly, and wastes money. This guide gives you a simple field method — the BTU/sqft rule — plus the factors that change the answer.

The Quick Answer: BTU per Square Foot

The fastest way to estimate AC size is to multiply conditioned floor area by a climate-based BTU/sqft factor:

Tons = (Area × BTU/sqft ÷ 12,000)

12,000 BTU/h = 1 ton of cooling

Example: 1,500 sqft home in Florida

Hot climate, west-facing: 30 BTU/sqft
1,500 × 30 = 45,000 BTU/h
Add: 8 windows (+8,000) + 4 people (+1,600) + 3 appliances (+1,500)
Subtotal: 56,100 BTU/h
Insulation credit (×0.85): 47,685 BTU/h
Tons: 47,685 ÷ 12,000 = 3.97 → 4 ton unit

Factors That Change AC Size

  • Climate & design temp — hotter regions need more tons per sqft
  • Insulation quality — R-30 roof/R-13 walls cut load by ~15%
  • Windows — each standard window adds ~1,000 BTU/h (west-facing worse)
  • Ceiling height — vaulted ceilings increase conditioned volume
  • Occupants & appliances — people add 400 BTU/h; major appliances 500 BTU/h each
  • Duct losses — leaky ducts in attics can add 20-30% to capacity needs
  • Sun exposure — west-facing walls and large unshaded windows increase load

Why Oversizing Is Bad

⚠️ Bigger is NOT better. Oversized units cool the space too quickly, then shut off (short cycling). Consequences: poor dehumidification (clammy air), temperature swings, higher bills, and premature compressor wear. Always size to the actual load.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many square feet will a 2-ton AC cool?

Typically 800-1,200 sqft, depending on climate and insulation. In hot climates, maybe 700 sqft; in mild climates, up to 1,200.

How do I calculate what size AC I need?

Multiply sqft by BTU/sqft factor (20-35 by climate), add corrections for windows/people/appliances, divide by 12,000 to get tons. For exact sizing, use full Manual J software.

Is it bad to oversize an air conditioner?

Yes — short cycling, poor dehumidification, temperature swings, higher bills, and compressor wear. Size to actual load.
